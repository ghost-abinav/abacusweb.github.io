Slider Abacus

A slider abacus is a variation of the traditional abacus that uses sliders instead of beads. These sliders, which are typically made of plastic, slide along rods or wires within a frame to represent numbers and perform calculations

Key features of a slider abacus: Sliders: Instead of beads, sliders are used to represent digits. Frame: The abacus has a frame with rods or wires where the sliders move. Calculation: Calculations are performed by moving the sliders to represent numbers and performing arithmetic operations. .
